Background

Hometown

I came from Hong Kong and it is a beautiful place. I have grown up and lived in a governmental building in Yau Tong(most middle-class people live in governmental buidling in Hong Kong because it is a populated city that is lack of lands, so it is impossible to own a single house like the US does here). Despite of that, it was a comfortable living place with many neighbors on a single floor(I lived on 16th floor). In addition to that, there was 3 shopping center around the building I lived, and there is a train station right beneath it. It was really convenient to go around back then, which I can never experiece once again in New York. Sometimes I misses my home, but I can no longer live there.

This is how it looks from outside of the ground floor of the building I lived in. I remember there is always a tall tree when I opened the front door and left the building.
This is the mini-park that is right outside of the back door of the building. I used to have a great time playing with my siblings there even it's just a small park.

Schools I had attended in Hong Kong

Pre-K: St. Antonius Kindergarten(SAK)

You may notice that SAK & SAPS sounds similar, and yes they are the sister schools sharing the building, except the school time is different. There is also a Secondary School named St. Antonius Girls' College(SAGC) for girls that is a few streets away.

Primary School(1st-6th grade): St. Antonius Primary School(SAPS)

Secondary School(7th grade: 2014 ~ 2015 summer): NLSI Lui Kwok Pat Fong College(LKPFC).
Even though I only attended this school for only 1 year because I immigrated to US after summer, this was the unforgettable year because my classmates and homeroom teachers held a party at school and gifted me a drawing & comments of classmates before I was gone. We also ate hotpot and decorated classroom at school as class activity. I always really cherish the memories.

The School I have attended in America after immigration

Middle School(8th grade: 2015 ~ 2016 summer): Intermediate School 96(IS 96 Seth Low). I already learned basic English in Hong Kong, so communication was not a problem and the school decide to put me in a regular class where there is no beginner/intermediate ESL students here. But the way I spoke English was pretty awkward when I first came here. Luckily, the people were nice and had high acceptance. So I gradually became open to my classmates.

High School(9th grade - 12th grade): James Madison High School(JMHS). I finally started to be comfortable talking with others when I was 10th grade, so most of the friends I made in America is from this school.

It is such a regret not to taking photos with them and teachers before COVID happened. JMHS figured a way to celebrate graduates in 2020, where they put all of the graduation photos in front of the school gates. That's pretty creative to be honest.
